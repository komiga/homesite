§ cover

I am a self-taught programmer and game maker with keen interest in system design, data, games, and narrative. I started programming at the age of 13 and have been primarily using Linux & C++ since 2009 & 2010, respectively.

I am an attentive & critical observer, with contributions to open-source projects such as Pygments, Elixir, Go, GLM, and libc++, among others. From 2012, I have volunteered QA for Linux-based video games, covering (to date) 19 games. In the same vein, I am a two-time participant of the game jam Ludum Dare, wherein much of my time is spent critiquing, breaking, and analyzing other participants' games. My less-visible community work spans the Wikimedia wikis and Project Gutenberg.

My adventures are diverse, with personal software projects involved in reverse-engineering, data collection & analysis, game systems, game emulation, graphics, mathematics, and networking. My professional experience includes software development, software repackaging & deployment, hardware configuration & deployment, and teaching.
