{! Section.item(C, "Quanta", "https://github.com/komiga/quanta") !}

An adaptive toolset for tracking… everything. Its primary facility is time-tracking, with a text format at its core specialized for describing the physical realm. Though Quanta is currently passive (with some validation), it will soon expand into analytics and prediction (e.g., identifying distraction, eager fact correlation, and nutrition).

I use Quanta to track every action I take (i.e., every second of every day), to track nutrition (to the gram & milligram), to spot-check vitals, to store my schedule & todo lists, and to write my journal.

It's a bit obsessive, but I see the ritual as a valuable lifelong science project. The overhead of tracking itself is mostly insignificant, and it decreases as I advance the assistive aspects of Quanta.

The most effort expended in tracking actually comes from transcribing immediate history. As of writing, Quanta is PC-bound, but I have plans for a pared-down cellphone app to track entries while I'm away from my PC. The present most significant assistive role Quanta has to play is in erasing this friction.

{! Section.item(C, "togo", "https://github.com/komiga/togo") !}

A C++ data-oriented super-library. It began as a game engine, but I found its design too appealing to leave it all bound up in a high-dependency library. As a result, togo is now composed of multiple components (libraries and tools). I am using it for all of my new projects, largely eschewing the C++ stdlib.

It is replacing many of the projects I started under earlier, poorer, design principles.
