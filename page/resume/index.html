I am Coranna Howard, a Seattle area-based programmer and game maker.

I started exploring programming at the age of 13, driven by my fascination & curiosity of the mechanisms behind games & software. Through reverse-engineering code and data formats from games, I built up a solid foundation in the practical & theoretical of computer science. This immersion also gave me critical attention to detail and a quirky, explorative style of interaction that tends to break everything (usually without even remotely intending to).

In the wider community, I have been an attentive & critical attendant, with contributions to open-source projects such as Pygments, Elixir, Go, GLM, and libc++, among others. From 2012, I have volunteered QA for Linux-based games, covering (to date) 19 games. In the same vein, I am a two-time participant of the game jam Ludum Dare. My less-visible community work spans the Wikimedia wikis and Project Gutenberg.

I am currently seeking remote (or very local) programming & system design work on games, game engines, and tools (in general), or consultation within that domain. Long term, I am seeking tech work on environmental and social betterment (preferably at a PBC/non-profit). I am also open to small (low or no budget) collaborative projects with independent craftspeople.
